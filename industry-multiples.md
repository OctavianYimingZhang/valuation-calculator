# Industry Multiples — Sector Benchmark Reference

## How to Use This Reference

For each sector, typical valuation ranges are provided for key multiples.
These are GUIDELINES, not hard rules. Actual multiples depend on:
- Growth rate (higher growth → higher multiple)
- Profitability (higher margins → higher multiple)
- Market conditions (bull market → higher multiples)
- Interest rates (lower rates → higher multiples)
- Company quality (moat, management → premium)

Always cross-reference with actual peer data when available.

## 1. Semiconductors & Semiconductor Equipment

**Companies**: TER (Teradyne), AEHR (Aehr Test Systems), TowerSemiconductor, KLAC, LRCX, AMAT

| Multiple | Low | Median | High | Notes |
|----------|-----|--------|------|-------|
| P/E (Forward) | 15x | 25x | 45x | Highly cyclical — use mid-cycle |
| EV/EBITDA | 10x | 18x | 30x | Capital-intensive |
| P/S | 3x | 6x | 12x | Varies by margin profile |
| P/B | 2x | 5x | 10x | Meaningful for fabs |
| P/FCF | 15x | 25x | 40x | Strong FCF generators |
| PEG | 0.8x | 1.3x | 2.0x | Use EPS CAGR 2-3Y |

**Key considerations:**
- Use mid-cycle earnings for P/E (smooth out cyclicality)
- Test intensity and TAM growth drive premium multiples
- AI/HPC exposure commands premium (per TER analysis)
- Customer concentration risk may warrant discount
- Memory cycle sensitive (HBM vs NAND pricing)

## 4. Mining / Resources / Uranium

**Companies**: UAMY (United States Antimony), UUUU (Energy Fuels), CCJ, UEC

| Multiple | Low | Median | High | Notes |
|----------|-----|--------|------|-------|
| P/E | 5x | 12x | 25x | Use normalized commodity prices |
| EV/EBITDA | 3x | 7x | 15x | Cyclical |
| P/NAV | 0.5x | 1.0x | 2.0x | Net Asset Value based on reserves |
| P/S | 1x | 3x | 8x | For producers |
| EV/Resource | varies | varies | varies | $/lb or $/oz in-ground |

**Key considerations:**
- NAV (Net Asset Value) based on proven reserves is primary
- Commodity price scenarios are essential
- Use EV/EBITDA at normalized commodity prices
- Production ramp-up vs exploration stage dramatically changes multiples
- Government policy (strategic stockpiling, tariffs) affects premium
- UAMY/UUUU pattern: critical minerals premium

## 5. Pre-Revenue / Early-Stage Technology

**Companies**: ACHR (Archer Aviation), ASTS (AST SpaceMobile)

| Multiple | Low | Median | High | Notes |
|----------|-----|--------|------|-------|
| EV/Backlog | 0.3x | 0.8x | 1.5x | If backlog exists |
| TAM Penetration | varies | varies | varies | Revenue potential |
| EV/Revenue (forward) | 5x | 15x | 50x+ | If near commercialization |

**Key considerations:**
- Traditional multiples do not apply (no revenue/earnings)
- Backlog-based: Total orders × delivery probability
- TAM analysis: Addressable market × penetration % × take rate
- Comparable transactions (M&A, funding rounds)
- Milestone-based valuation (FAA cert, first delivery, etc.)
- Extremely wide scenario ranges needed (bear case may approach zero)
- ACHR pattern: In-hand orders ($6B) as valuation floor
- ASTS pattern: Contract values + government contracts as anchor

|----------|-----|--------|------|-------|

| EV/Revenue | 3x | 10x | 25x | SaaS-like recurring |

- AI infrastructure exposure commands significant premium

- IREN pattern: Mining + AI cloud hybrid requires blended approach
- COMM pattern: Turnaround story, leverage matters, watch debt reduction
